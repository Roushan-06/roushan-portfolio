Project Overview

SKIG Energies needed a better way to generate genuine solar enquiries. Before this funnel, the client had tested multiple marketing approaches but was receiving many low-quality leads. Some people filled forms casually, some submitted by mistake, and many enquiries were not serious enough for the sales team to spend time on.

The goal was not just to generate more leads. The goal was to generate better-qualified solar leads by making prospects share useful information before reaching the sales team.

Calculate Your Solar Budget in 30 Seconds.

The Problem

  1. Junk leads from basic forms: people were submitting forms without real buying intent.
  2. Low-quality enquiries: the sales team did not have enough information to understand whether the person was actually fit for solar installation.
  3. Manual qualification burden: the team had to spend time calling leads who were not serious, not eligible, or did not have the right property conditions.

The old funnel was collecting leads, but it was not helping the sales team identify who was worth following up with.

My Strategy

Instead of creating another simple lead form, I planned a qualification-first funnel. The idea was to make the user take a small but meaningful action before becoming a lead.

The calculator collected key solar-fit details such as:

  • Monthly electricity bill
  • Property type
  • Property ownership confirmation
  • Rooftop availability
  • Roof shadow condition
  • Contact details
  • Address, area, and pincode
  • Preferred language

The page also explained that users could receive details such as recommended solar system size, estimated project cost, subsidy eligibility guidance, and approximate payback period. This helped the funnel educate the prospect and qualify the lead before sales follow-up.

What I Built

1. Calculator-Based Landing Page

I created a landing page that did not feel like a boring lead form. It gave users a clear reason to submit their details: they would get a solar budget estimate based on their inputs.

2. Lead Qualification Structure

The funnel was designed to filter users based on important solar-fit factors such as ownership and rooftop availability. This reduced irrelevant enquiries and gave the sales team better information before making a call.

3. Zoho Pipeline Integration

After form submission, lead data was connected to the client's Zoho pipeline. This helped the team manage leads in a structured way instead of losing enquiries across different channels.

4. Follow-Up System

The funnel was supported with automated follow-up communication through WhatsApp, email, and SMS. Messaging focused on solar buying triggers like electricity savings, long-term cost reduction, EMI affordability, rooftop benefits, and the next-step consultation.

Why This Funnel Worked Better

A basic lead form only collects name and phone number. This funnel collected buying context. That means the client could understand whether the person had a high enough electricity bill, owned the property, had rooftop space, had a suitable roof, and was worth immediate follow-up.

Result

  • Improved lead quality
  • Created a clearer sales follow-up process
  • Connected lead data directly into Zoho pipeline
  • Helped the sales team focus on more serious prospects
  • Created a scalable funnel that can be used for paid campaigns
